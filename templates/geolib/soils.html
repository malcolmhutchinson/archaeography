Soil science data

Starting in the 1930s, The Department of Scientific and Industrial Research (DSIR) conducted a series of soil surveys in the Waikato basin and surrounding hill country. It was these soil scientists who first recognised the Maori-made soils along the Waikato and Waipa rivers.

The surveys were mapped, and eventually converted into polygon geometries and are now available as GIS vector files from Landcare Research Manaaki Whenua, through their Land Resource Information Systems (LRIS) Portal.

We are most interested in these soil type datasets because they describe anthropic, or human-made, soils. That is, the class of soil called Tamahere loam is recognised as being made by Maori, and that makes the soil deposits archaeological.

We've taken a copy of some of the soil type data and identified Maori-made soils in them as a special subset. We use this modified dataset to analyse archaeological records.

A current research project is to estimate the area of land under cultivation by Maori. The soil scientists mapped 2,799.7 ha of anthropic soils across the four Waikato valley surveys.
